Frequently Asked Questions

Does this compromise the privacy and freedom of my workers/customers?

Actually, the opposite. You are directly contributing to everyone's safety and indirectly contributing to your own by using verifiable credentials: you are also increasing your privacy beyond what privacy regulations in most places require. This exceeds what companies are required by law to do today! It is a consent-based system. Your workers/customers have the freedom to share your information to a valid inspector or refuse, on a case-by-case basis.


Immunodex contributes to your workers/customers' safety by letting policy makers in a company or government know about the health of their people in a timely and reliable manner. In order to do this, the "paper trail" of the health records must be traceable. This trail rightfully belongs to the individual. But right now, it does not. It sits on clinics' computers or the hospital's servers, or the government has it. You have no access to your own health data, but we let you hold your own data. Finally, the inspection is not only consent-based, but requires a minimum exchange of information, which means your workers/customers might not need to reveal ALL data, but simply fulfil the requirements of you the inspector (this is "zero-knowledge proof").

We don't know about the immunology, whether having antibodies actually confers long term immunity to Covid-19. Also, the antibody test market is full of scams!

Yes, you are right. And it doesn't matter. Immunodex is a bias-free, completely neutral historian that records personal health data for that person. Immunodex is starting off with use cases concerning Covid-19 because that is important and urgent. New research and findings do not invalidate records on Immunodex. In fact, reliable records contribute to ongoing research.

What is blockchain (distributed ledger), and how does it work? Why do we need it?

Reuters has done a great job of distilling what blockchain is in this illustrated article.

Simply put, distributed ledger technology has trust, security and privacy baked-in. Lack of these three features is the greatest problem facing our current ways of handling sensitive health information.

Do you store any information?

No, Immunodex does not store any information.

Most other solutions out there are built using a conventional database structure, which you can imagine as a honeypot full of valuable personal (health-related) information. Naturally, this honeypot attracts hackers if there is a payload inside. Even if encrypted, hackers can figure out ways to breach the database, and resell the information or use it for malicious purposes.

Immunodex does not rely on databases. Rather, our system is built on a cryptographically-secured distributed ledger (a.k.a. blockchain), meaning there is no single centralized database (the "honeypot") for hackers to breach. Moreover, what's stored on the distributed ledger is hashed data, which cannot be un-hashed (made into usable information) without your private key that is specific to your own user account. Hence, your information is stored only with you (until you consent to share it with an external party), and you are the guardian of your own data.

What happens if I got a result from a test kit that was later recalled/nullified by the government?

Your record remains with you forever. Immunodex is independent of policies from government or authorities. Inspectors requesting proof-of-health will see not only the result but also that the test kit used is or is not on the [currently] approved list of test kits.

What happens if I lose/break my phone? How do I access the "backup?"

The patient or authorized person (“Holder”) primarily holds the information on their computer or mobile device(s), and the information should be backed up (location is the Holder's choice). A back-up is an encrypted file that is an exact copy of the one on the main device(s). The Holder accesses their information (working copy and backups) via a key. If the key is lost, then the patient undergoes a Key Recovery Process, which is similar to the onboarding process.

What is the difference between you and the Canadian government's COVID Alert app?

Covid ALERT is not designed for institutional contact-tracing. For example, institutions cannot actively prevent risky or "non-compliant" people from entering their premises. Covid ALERT is solely meant to alert individuals when they have been exposed to a positive-testing person (the status of whom must be voluntarily self-uploaded).

Immunodex is meant for institutions (such as schools and businesses) who need to monitor the health of their clients, staff and teams in a private and secure way. We help prevent outbreaks before they can occur. Please contact us to see a demo

This is not to say that Covid ALERT is useless. We see Immunodex as being complementary to contact-tracing apps, as another weapon in the arsenal to protect ourselves from outbreaks. However, we make sure that our usage of data in this fight is verifiable, private and responsible.

Immunodex is an initiative combining industry experts and technologists in healthcare and cryptography to build a global standard for health records based on the W3C Verifiable Credentials model and the Pan-Canadian Trust Framework.
